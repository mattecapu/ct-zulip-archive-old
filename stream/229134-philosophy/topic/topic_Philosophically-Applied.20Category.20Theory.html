David Corfield (Mar 20 2023 at 11:42):

I'm looking to form a research network, called something like PhACT (Philosophically-Applied Category Theory), aimed at encouraging dialogue between the ACT and philosophical communities. So, for instance, where there is plenty of ACT work looking to capture causal relations in causal network, there is also decades of work in philosophy on causality, e.g., discussion on different conceptions in Regularity theory, Probabilistic theory, Counterfactual theory, Interventionist theory, Process theory,... The idea then is for some mutually beneficial exchange, where philosophy gains by taking on a powerful formalism. Anyone interested in such a network?

David Corfield (Mar 21 2023 at 10:02):

I'll be looking to apply for some money from my side for networks and research time. Access to non-Humanities funding would certainly be welcome.

I had a really interesting chat with Evan Patterson yesterday. Plenty of overlap there. I really don't think parts of the shared activity need to look ever so different from just doing ACT.

One dimension that certainly interests me is a tension between philosophers as to whether scientific theories and scientific inference should be seen as neat or messy. More attention these days is paid to the messy "middle-range theory", e.g., epidemiology, ecology. Here there's a robust tangle of practices (for the messy tangle, see p. 313 of Nancy Cartwright's ' Middle-range theory: Without it what could anyone do?' https://ojs.ehu.eus/index.php/THEORIA/article/download/21479/19859).

The compositionality inherent in the ACT approaches to networks might provide just the right tools.
