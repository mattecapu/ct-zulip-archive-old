The category of pure numerical motives is known to be abelian and semisimple, while for the pure Chow motives in my talk this is still just conjectured.

John Baez (May 27 2022 at 05:58):

John Baez (May 27 2022 at 06:00):

To do this he defines a Weil ppp-number to be a number whose Galois conjugates, say α\alphaα are all such that ∣α∣=pk/2|\alpha| = p^{k/2}∣α∣=pk/2 for some kkk and also αpn\alpha p^n αpn is an algebraic integer for big enough nnn.

John Baez (May 27 2022 at 06:01):

This is the kind of α\alphaα we've been talking about, which shows up in the Riemann Hypothesis for finite fields. But I'm listing more conditions on it now!

John Baez (May 27 2022 at 06:05):

Anyway, Milne shows that simple objects in the category of pure numerical motives correspond to equivalence classes of Weil ppp-numbers... where two are equivalent if they're in the same orbit of the absolute Galois group! (That's what I meant by "Galois conjugates" above.)

John Baez (May 27 2022 at 06:06):

So this is great: it means the α\alphaα's I've been talking about, or at least equivalence classes of them, really are the same as the basic building blocks in the world of motives, at least if we use pure numerical motives.

John Baez (May 28 2022 at 15:48):

Actually he does all this assuming the Tate conjecture. Wikipedia says:

Like the Hodge conjecture, the Tate conjecture would imply most of Grothendieck's standard conjectures on algebraic cycles. Namely, it would imply the Lefschetz standard conjecture (that the inverse of the Lefschetz isomorphism is defined by an algebraic correspondence); that the Künneth components of the diagonal are algebraic; and that numerical equivalence and homological equivalence of algebraic cycles are the same.
