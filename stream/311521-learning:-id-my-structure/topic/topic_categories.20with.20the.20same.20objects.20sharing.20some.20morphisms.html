Topic: categories with the same objects sharing some morphisms

{% raw %}

Carlos Zapata-Carratala (Aug 12 2022 at 11:03):

Consider two categories AAA and BBB over the same family of objects Ob(A)=Ob(B)\text{Ob}(A)=\text{Ob}(B)Ob(A)=Ob(B). Consider the subset of (pairs of) morphisms in AAA that are splittings, i.e. a morphism with a one-sided inverse, which form a subcategory, S⊂AS\subset AS⊂A. What I have found is the situation where there is a faithful functor i:S→Bi: S \to Bi:S→B, so informally one can think that there are two categories over the same objects sharing some morphisms, i.e. Hom(A)∩Hom(B)=Hom(S)\text{Hom}(A) \cap \text{Hom}(B) = \text{Hom}(S)Hom(A)∩Hom(B)=Hom(S), but otherwise they may not interact further. Any ideas about theory on this sort of situation?

Mike Shulman (Aug 12 2022 at 17:15):

An [[M-category]] is two categories with the same set of objects and where the morphisms of one are included in the morphisms of the other. This can be formalized as a category enriched over the cartesian monoidal category M\mathcal{M}M whose objects are injections of sets and whose morphisms are commutative squares. Something like your situation could probably be formulated similarly in terms of enrichment over a category whose objects are spans of injections.

dusko (Aug 13 2022 at 00:03):

sorry if i am being thick, carlos, but it seems to me that there are two ways to interpret what you write:
Carlos Zapata-Carratala said:

Consider the subset of (pairs of) morphisms in AAA that are splittings, i.e. a morphism with a one-sided inverse, which form a subcategory, S⊂AS\subset AS⊂A.

there are two kinds of morphisms with one-sided inverses: split epis and split monos.

if we take all morphisms with one-sided inverses, as you seem to be suggesting, the spanned subcategory SSS consists of the morphisms that have a split epi-split mono factorization.

if we assume that you forgot to mention that you are looking only, say, at the morphisms with a right inverse, then the subcategory SSS is simply the subcategory of split epis in AAA. (this is the case where john power's categories enriched over sets with subsets applies, to which mike shulman seems to refer.)

in the first case, the subcategory SSS seems like the largest absolutely complete subcategory of AAA. if SSS is also the largest absolutely complete core of BBB, then the functors out of AAA and BBB must coincide on this part but may differ on the rest.

in the second case, if AAA and BBB share the same split epis, but the actual splittings are different then this might be some interesting AC subtlety, of two universes with different choice functions and different well-orderings...

i am sure that i am way off in at least one of the two cases, but both are interesting, so thank you :)
