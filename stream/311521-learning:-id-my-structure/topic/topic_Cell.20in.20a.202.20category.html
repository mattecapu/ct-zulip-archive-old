Reading about a variant of the conduche condition in "Lax Kan extensions between Double Categories" and realized this diagram summarizes it in a case I'm interested in. I want to know if there's a good way to think about this, or if anyone recognizes it. It's pretty simple so I am hoping someone recognizes it.

i am working in the 2 category Cat in what follows.
If (q:a→b,s:a→c)(q:a\to b, s:a\to c)(q:a→b,s:a→c) is a span in Cat, by its 2-limit I mean a category eee with functors x:e→b,y:e→a,z:e→cx:e\to b, y:e\to a, z:e\to cx:e→b,y:e→a,z:e→c and natural transformations α:x→qy\alpha : x\to qyα:x→qy, β:sy→z\beta :sy\to zβ:sy→z, which is terminal among such cones.

Now say I have a square of functors connected by a 2 cell α:pq⇒rs\alpha : pq\Rightarrow rsα:pq⇒rs.

Then there is a canonical functor from the 2-limit of the span (q,s)(q, s) (q,s) to the comma category of the cospan (p,r)(p, r) (p,r).

The conduche condition I am interested in says that this functor is surjective and that objects have essentially unique lifts up to isomorphism, perhaps it would be more natural to strengthen this to an equivalence so the limit of the span is equivalent to the limit of the cospan in a sense.
Is anything known about 2- cells with this property?
