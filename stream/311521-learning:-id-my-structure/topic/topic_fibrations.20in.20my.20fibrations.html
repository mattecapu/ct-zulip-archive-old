James Deikun (Jan 20 2023 at 22:19):

Okay I made a serious mistake earlier: pullbacks don't suffice to have pullback functors for, say, !:2→1!: 2 \to 1!:2→1 in Fam(C)\mathrm{Fam}(C)Fam(C) be fibrations because the definition of fibration is too strict. Pullback functors like !∗!^{*}!∗ are some kind of lax Street fibrations described below. I think "sub-unary pullback" is still a decent word for what you get when pullback functors for monos are fibrations though--you just don't need the laxness, or even Streetness, in this case.

James Deikun (Jan 20 2023 at 22:32):

Lax Street fibrations: Consider a functor p:E→Bp : E \to Bp:E→B. Call ϕ:e′→e,h:p(e′)→b\phi : e' \to e, h : p(e') \to bϕ:e′→e,h:p(e′)→b a lax Cartesian lift of f:b→p(e)f : b \to p(e)f:b→p(e) if p(ϕ)=f∘hp(\phi) = f \circ hp(ϕ)=f∘h and whenever ψ:e′′→e\psi : e'' \to eψ:e′′→e in EEE and g:p(e′′)→bg : p(e'') \to bg:p(e′′)→b in BBB such that f∘g=p(ψ)f \circ g = p(\psi)f∘g=p(ψ), there exists a unique χ:e′′→e′\chi : e'' \to e'χ:e′′→e′ such that ψ=ϕ∘χ\psi = \phi \circ \chiψ=ϕ∘χ and h∘p(χ)=gh \circ p(\chi) = gh∘p(χ)=g. ppp is a Lax street fibration if every arrow in BBB has a lax Cartesian lift.
