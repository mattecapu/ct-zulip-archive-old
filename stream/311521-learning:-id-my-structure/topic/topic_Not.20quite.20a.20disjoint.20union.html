Mike Stay (May 21 2023 at 06:52):

Motivation: in Objects of Categories as Complex Numbers, @Marcelo Fiore and @Tom Leinster show how to give cardinalities to objects equipped with isomorphisms. When considering the category of countable sets, there are the finite sets with cardinalities in N\mathbb{N}N, and there are certain infinite sets with cardinalities in C\mathbb{C}C. For example, the set TTT of binary trees is equipped with the isomorphism T≅T2+1T \cong T^2 + 1T≅T2+1, so it has cardinality 1+3i2\frac{1+\sqrt{3}i}{2}21+3​i​.

The cardinalities of finite sets are called "low" elements of the resulting ring RRR of cardinalities, and the others are called "high". The ring RRR has additive and multiplicative identities low 0 and low 1 on the whole ring, and additive and multiplicative idempotents high 0 and high 1 that are identities on the high elements. High 0 is isomorphic to T3+1T^3 + 1T3+1 and high 1 is isomorphic to T6T^6T6. Adding high 0 to a low element lifts it to the inclusion of N\mathbb{N}N in C\mathbb{C}C; similarly, multiplying by high 1 lifts a low element to its inclusion.

Generalizing a little, we have nnn rings R1⊆R2⊆⋯⊆RnR_1 \subseteq R_2 \subseteq \cdots \subseteq R_nR1​⊆R2​⊆⋯⊆Rn​ and we get a new ring with the disjoint union of the elements where the axioms of RiR_iRi​ apply to the elements of RjR_jRj​ where j≥ij \ge ij≥i. So if n=5n=5n=5, 0 and 1 in R3R_3R3​ are identities for R3,R4,R_3, R_4,R3​,R4​, and R5R_5R5​, but are merely idempotents for R1R_1R1​ and R2R_2R2​.

Generalizing some more, we have nnn models of a geometric theory with inclusions between them. The result is a model of a geometric theory where axioms hold "for all elements except the ones in lower models".

Is there a name for this construction?
