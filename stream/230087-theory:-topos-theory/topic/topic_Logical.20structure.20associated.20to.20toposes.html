Kobe Wullaert (Jul 20 2023 at 11:51):

Hi everyone
Every (elementary) topos induces some logical structure, e.g.,:

  • a (first-order) hyperdoctrine; by mapping an object to the Heyting algebra of its subobjects;
  • a model of type theory, such as a category with attributes (or a comprehension structure); because a topos is locally cartesian closed.

My question is whether this connection has been studied in more detail, e.g., whether it possible to recognize (characterize) which hyperdoctrines/comprehension categories arise from a (elementary) topos?
