Callan McGill (Nov 07 2022 at 15:24):

Does anyone know of something that describes some of the theory of indexed adjunctions? I have in mind something like the situation of Fam(Set) and Fam(Vect) where there is a fiberwise indexed adjunction between the two.

Jade Master (Nov 07 2022 at 16:16):

Given a category C, there is a 2-category [C,Cat] of functors from C into Cat, natural transformations between functors, and modifications between natural transformations. An adjunction internal to this 2-category is the same thing as an indexed family of adjunctions between the fibers.

Jade Master (Nov 07 2022 at 16:26):

And then also there's an equivalence [C,Cat]≅Fib(C)[C,Cat] \cong Fib(C)[C,Cat]≅Fib(C) via the Grothendieck construction. The equivalence will preserve adjunctions, so it's saying that you can glue the fibrewise adjunctions into a single adjunction on their Grothendieck constructions.
