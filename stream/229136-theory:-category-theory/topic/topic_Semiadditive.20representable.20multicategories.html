Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Dec 04 2022 at 12:01):

Has anyone worked on this topic?

I'm looking for a categorical doctrine where I have:

  • tensor products
  • multilinear morphisms
  • binary products
  • a structure of monoid on the hom-sets

The first two requirements give the notion of representable multicategory, the two last ones give the notion of semiadditive category (ie. a category with binary biproducts, equivalently binary products and enrichment in commutative monoids, I don't care about zero objects).

In such a context, you will have linear maps, multilinear maps + other maps (polynomial maps?), for instance in the category of vector spaces you should be able to form this map:

X⊕X→X→X⊕n→X⊗nX \oplus X \rightarrow X \rightarrow X^{\oplus n} \rightarrow X^{\otimes n}X⊕X→X→X⊕n→X⊗n
(a,b)↦a+b↦(a+b,...,a+b)↦(a+b)⊗n(a,b) \mapsto a + b \mapsto (a+b,...,a+b) \mapsto (a+b)^{\otimes n}(a,b)↦a+b↦(a+b,...,a+b)↦(a+b)⊗n

(the weird map X⊕n→X⊗nX^{\oplus n} \rightarrow X^{\otimes n}X⊕n→X⊗n is something offered by representable multicategories. Edit: no, that's false.)

I'm not exactly sure how to put all of this together.

Graham Manuell (Dec 04 2022 at 14:50):

To me this sounds like a semiadditive monoidal category. In the terminology of the nlab it would be a [[distributive monoidal category]] with finite biproducts. Well, except that you don't want a zero object. Once you have tensor products, you don't need to worry about multilinear maps anymore.

Jean-Baptiste Vienney (Dec 04 2022 at 15:00):

Not exactly, you can't talk of the map a↦a⊗na \mapsto a^{\otimes n}a↦a⊗n in a monoidal category! because you crucially need the map X⊕n→X⊗n X^{\oplus n} \rightarrow X^{\otimes n}X⊕n→X⊗n! I thought this me too for a long time before realizing that you can't do everything with tensor products as weird as it seems. Such a map is obtained by combining a linear map followed by a multilinear map and there is no way of doing this with just a monoidal category.
