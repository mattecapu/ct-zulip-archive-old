Say we have a specific "operad with shapes" OOO. Then the "framed thickening" T(O)T(O)T(O) should roughly consist of:

  1. Shapes inherited from OOO.
  2. New shapes that come from the operations of OOO.   4. New compositions that come from the associativity equations of OOO. These at least make the new kkk-ary operations above into an unbiased associative "operation".
  5. New associativity equations that the above compositions should satisfy by construction.
  6. Some rules for existence of "units" and "restrictions" TBD.

Where do the degeneracies come from? For virtual double categories, the degeneracies among just squares look like the degeneracies of the simplex category, 1, 2, 3 as you go up one-by-one in arity, C(n,k)C(n,k)C(n,k) as you skip kkk many; however there are extra generators: from a point you get a degenerate looping arrow or proarrow, from an arrow a degenerate nullary square with the arrow on both sides and a degenerate target, from a proarrow a degenerate unary square the shape of an identity square for it.

For virtually-thickened strict ω\omegaω-cats, you get, in the cylinders, a copy of the sensible degeneracies for globular pasting diagrams, plus from any globe you get its "identity cylinder" shape, and its "reflexive globe" shape, plus you get degeneracies (of cylinders again) that correspond to using reflexivity on the target. The shape category of an "operad with shapes" OOO is a direct category S(O)S(O)S(O) with finite fan-in. The operations can be viewed as a category Op(O)\mathrm{Op}(O)Op(O) equipped with a discrete fibration cod:Op(O)→S(O)\mathrm{cod} : \mathrm{Op}(O) \to S(O)cod:Op(O)→S(O) and a profunctor dom:Op(O)⇸S(O)\mathrm{dom} : \mathrm{Op}(O) ⇸ S(O)dom:Op(O)⇸S(O) such that the collage of the whole diagram (and thus also Op(O)\mathrm{Op}(O)Op(O)) is direct with finite fan-in. The specifications exist as the arrows in Op(O)\mathrm{Op}(O)Op(O) and the action of the profunctor dom\mathrm{dom}dom.

•, Δ, and the standard multicategories

In order to talk about the objects of a T-multicategory, you need tools a little more sensitive than the global point 111. The first of these tools is the dot, ∙\bullet∙. This is a subterminal object of E\mathcal{E}E which induces an idempotent modality ♭\flat♭ via Cartesian product. This "flat" modality forgets everything but the "objects" or "dots" in an E\mathcal{E}E-object.

The second tool is the degeneracy monad, Δ\DeltaΔ. This serves as a non-idempotent modality that builds up where "flat" tears down. Semantically, it serves to add "degenerate cells" that reflect lower-dimensional things, including objects, into the higher dimensions of E\mathcal{E}E. In good cases, at least, Δ\DeltaΔ is stable, strongly Cartesian, and left adjoint, but which of these nice attributes are essential remains to be determined. Δ\DeltaΔ must obey the law Δ∙=1\Delta\bullet = 1Δ∙=1.

The third tool is the virtual distributive law S\mathbb{S}S (of TTT over Δ\DeltaΔ). It serves as compatibility data between Δ\DeltaΔ and TTT, by deciding when a T-term with some degenerate arguments is actually a degenerate version of another T-term. In the case when T is the free category monad, it decides when a "slow path" that can pause at dots traverses the same edges as a "fast path", or when it doesn't traverse any "real" edges at all.

S\mathbb{S}S is a functor from E\mathcal{E}E to itself, with two natural projections, domS\mathrm{dom}_{\mathbb{S}}domS​ to TΔT\DeltaTΔ and codS\mathrm{cod}_{\mathbb{S}}codS​ to ΔT\Delta{}TΔT.
