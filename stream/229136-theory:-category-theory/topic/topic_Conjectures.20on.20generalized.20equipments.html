It seems like there ought to be some definition of "operad with shapes" and "framed thickening" that allows generalizations of some classic definitions and results using categories and equipments. An "operad with shapes" should basically be:

  1. A category of shapes S(O)S(O)S(O).
  2. Operations ooo, each of which has a domain dom(o)\mathrm{dom}(o)dom(o) consisting of multiple shapes pasted together, and a codomain cod(o)\mathrm{cod}(o)cod(o) consisting of a single shape, and specifications for each boundary facet of cod(o)\mathrm{cod}(o)cod(o) identifying it as o′(a,b,c,d,...)o'(a,b,c,d,...)o′(a,b,c,d,...) where a,b,c,d,...a,b,c,d,...a,b,c,d,... are appropriate facets of dom(o)\mathrm{dom}(o)dom(o).
  3. Compositions, equations of the form o(o′(a,b,...),o′′(c,d,...),...)=p(a,b,c,d,...)o(o'(a,b,...),o''(c,d,...),...)=p(a,b,c,d,...)o(o′(a,b,...),o′′(c,d,...),...)=p(a,b,c,d,...) (nesting depth is arbitrary). Compositions are "dependent" on whichever other compositions are needed to make them well-typed.
  4. Associativity of compositions. This can be expressed in such a way that the RHS is always a simple composition and the LHS is a "composition of compositions", which is important.
  5. Maybe some scaffolding needed to identify what "units" and "restrictions" are.

James Deikun (Dec 28 2022 at 16:01):

Say we have a specific "operad with shapes" OOO. Then the "framed thickening" T(O)T(O)T(O) should roughly consist of:

  1. Shapes inherited from OOO.
  2. New shapes that come from the operations of OOO. The new shape for ooo has cod(o)\mathrm{cod}(o)cod(o) as "front", dom(o)\mathrm{dom}(o)dom(o) as "back", and as "sides" the new shapes for specifications of ooo glued to their arguments in "back" and corresponding boundary cell in "front". Note that since every shape has at least an identity operation, you get at least one new "thick" version of each inherited shape.
  3. New operations that come from the compositions of OOO. The domain comes from the LHS, the codomain from the RHS, and the specifications from the dependencies. The compositions of multiply-nested identities give at least an kkk-ary operation for each kkk on the new "simply thick" cells.
  4. New compositions that come from the associativity equations of OOO. These at least make the new kkk-ary operations above into an unbiased associative "operation".
  5. New associativity equations that the above compositions should satisfy by construction.
  6. Some rules for existence of "units" and "restrictions" TBD.

James Deikun (Dec 29 2022 at 17:17):

Where do the degeneracies come from? For virtual double categories, the degeneracies among just squares look like the degeneracies of the simplex category, 1, 2, 3 as you go up one-by-one in arity, C(n,k)C(n,k)C(n,k) as you skip kkk many; however there are extra generators: from a point you get a degenerate looping arrow or proarrow, from an arrow a degenerate nullary square with the arrow on both sides and a degenerate target, from a proarrow a degenerate unary square the shape of an identity square for it.

For virtually-thickened strict ω\omegaω-cats, you get, in the cylinders, a copy of the sensible degeneracies for globular pasting diagrams, plus from any globe you get its "identity cylinder" shape, and its "reflexive globe" shape, plus you get degeneracies (of cylinders again) that correspond to using reflexivity on the target. I can see how the former matches up as special cases of this when you limit to 0- and 1-globes as shapes.

For the virtual thickening of the trinary-operation planar operad ("odd multicategories"), the shapes are objects plus corollas with an odd number of in-leaves. For degeneracies it seems like you get: the identity shape from objects, tripling an in-leaf, plus three ways of tripling the output and bending two copies back. This is a nice example since the original operad has no (room for) nontrivial degeneracies among shapes so these must all arise from operations, compositions and/or dependencies somehow.

James Deikun (Dec 31 2022 at 14:31):

The shape category of an "operad with shapes" OOO is a direct category S(O)S(O)S(O) with finite fan-in. The operations can be viewed as a category Op(O)\mathrm{Op}(O)Op(O) equipped with a discrete fibration cod:Op(O)→S(O)\mathrm{cod} : \mathrm{Op}(O) \to S(O)cod:Op(O)→S(O) and a profunctor dom:Op(O)⇸S(O)\mathrm{dom} : \mathrm{Op}(O) ⇸ S(O)dom:Op(O)⇸S(O) such that the collage of the whole diagram (and thus also Op(O)\mathrm{Op}(O)Op(O)) is direct with finite fan-in. The specifications exist as the arrows in Op(O)\mathrm{Op}(O)Op(O) and the action of the profunctor dom\mathrm{dom}dom.

So far what has been specified is more like a "collection with shapes" than an "operad with shapes". Given two such collections X,YX, YX,Y with the same shapes SSS, there is a monoidal product where the operations of X∘YX \circ YX∘Y are operations xxx of XXX equipped with consistent labellings of dom(−,x)\mathrm{dom}(-,x)dom(−,x) by operations of YYY. "Consistent" means that: you have a SSS-set morphism from dom(−,x)\mathrm{dom}(-,x)dom(−,x) to Op(Y)\mathrm{Op}(Y)Op(Y) (viewed as a presheaf via codY\mathrm{cod}_YcodY​). codX∘Y\mathrm{cod}_{X \circ Y}codX∘Y​ is basically inherited from XXX and domX∘Y\mathrm{dom}_{X \circ Y}domX∘Y​ is obtained from domY\mathrm{dom}_YdomY​ pointwise by gluing along the category of elements of dom(−,x)\mathrm{dom}(-,x)dom(−,x). (A concern: does this actually exist in all cases? I think it does though since it looks pointwise like a colimit in a presheaf category...) Then an operad with shapes SSS is a monoid in this monoidal category.

(It would be nice to have a form of this definition that didn't do so much equivocation between presheaves and discrete fibrations.)
