Topic: Fib has conjoints but not companions

Christian Williams (Dec 09 2022 at 00:43):

A fibration A→I\mathbb{A}\to \mathbb{I}A→I is a "dependent category" : over each object III there is a category AI\mathbb{A}_IAI​, and over each morphism f:I→Jf:I\to Jf:I→J there is a profunctor which is representable a conjoint AI(1,f∗):AI→AJ\mathbb{A}_I(1,f^\ast):\mathbb{A}_I\to \mathbb{A}_JAI​(1,f∗):AI​→AJ​ : a representation is a choice of cartesian morphisms.

Christian Williams (Dec 09 2022 at 00:43):

So, a fibration is essentially a diagram of conjoints in Cat, whose collage gives back the total category A\mathbb{A}A.
