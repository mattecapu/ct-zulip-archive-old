Christian Williams (Dec 12 2022 at 21:59):

The first picture is a square in the arrow double category of Cat:

  • objects are functors (X→X0X\to X_0X→X0​ etc)
  • tight/verticals are commuting squares (f,f0)(f,f_0)(f,f0​);
  • loose/horizontals are transformations (Q⇒Q0Q\Rightarrow Q_0Q⇒Q0​), and
  • squares are commutative squares of transformations (whole picture).

Christian Williams (Dec 12 2022 at 22:04):

In the second picture:

  • an object is an "indexed category" (a lax functor from a category into Cat, X:X0→CatX:X_0\to \mathrm{Cat}X:X0​→Cat);
  • a tight/vertical is an indexed functor (a lax transformation f:f0→Catfunf:f_0\to \mathrm{Cat}_{fun}f:f0​→Catfun​);
  • a loose/horizontal is an indexed profunctor (a "bimodule" of lax functors Q:Q0→CatprofQ:Q_0\to \mathrm{Cat}_{prof}Q:Q0​→Catprof​)
  • a square is an indexed transformation (a transformation of bimodules, the whole picture)
