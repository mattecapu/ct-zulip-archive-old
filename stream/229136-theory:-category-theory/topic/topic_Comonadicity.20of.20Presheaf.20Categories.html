Since comonadic functors create colimits a simple consequence would be that colimits in the presheaf cat are computed pointwise. And if it's also the case that presheaf categories are monadic with the corresponding left adjoint this would also give a reason why limits are given pointwise.

Mike Shulman (May 15 2023 at 16:13):

One consequence that I find interesting and useful is that when you consider Cat-enrichment, the category of 2-presheaves has both lax/pseudo morphism classifiers (because it's finitary monadic) and co-classifiers (because it's co-finitary co-monadic). Thus, for Cat-valued presheaves X,YX,YX,Y there are both QXQXQX and RYRYRY such that a pseudonatural map X⇝YX \leadsto YX⇝Y can be represented either by a strict map QX→YQX\to YQX→Y or a strict map X→RYX\to RYX→RY. This categorifies to "bar and cobar constructions" in homotopy theory.
