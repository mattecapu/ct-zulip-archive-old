Is the pushout of monos a mono in the category Cat\mathsf{Cat}Cat of small categories ? Is Cat\mathsf{Cat}Cat adhesive?

Nasos Evangelou-Oost (Nov 20 2021 at 15:47):

I think the answer to both is "no": https://www.ioc.ee/~pawel/papers/adhesive.pdf

Nasos Evangelou-Oost (Nov 20 2021 at 16:15):

What about for the category of monoids Mon\mathsf{Mon}Mon?

Evan Patterson (Nov 20 2021 at 19:19):

That's right: the coprojections of a pushout of monics in Cat are not necessarily monic, and thus Cat is not adhesive.

Some results, counterexamples, and references about the first problem are in this paper: https://doi.org/10.4153/CMB-2009-030-5

Reid Barton (Nov 20 2021 at 22:20):

You can freely invert a morphism f:x→yf : x \to yf:x→y of a category CCC by forming the pushout of the functor {0→1}→C\{0 \to 1\} \to C{0→1}→C picking out fff with the inclusion {0→1}→{0≅1}\{0 \to 1\} \to \{0 \cong 1\}{0→1}→{0≅1}. Those are both monomorphisms if x≠yx \ne yx=y (if x=yx = yx=y so fff is an endomorphism, then you can push out along functor BN→BZB \mathbb{N} \to B \mathbb{Z}BN→BZ instead, which is also a monomorphism).

Reid Barton (Nov 20 2021 at 22:21):

Now we could construct an example like: take the category with three objects AAA, BBB, CCC, two distinct parallel maps fff, g:A→Bg : A \to Bg:A→B and a map h:B→Ch : B \to Ch:B→C with hf=hghf = hghf=hg. Then if we invert hhh, we cause fff and ggg to become equal so the map to the localization is not a mono.

John Baez (Nov 20 2021 at 22:34):

Nice, thanks!

With a suitable pushout along a mono we can invert any morphism hhh in a category, so we had hf=hghf = hghf=hg in that category, we now have f=gf = gf=g in the pushout, even if it wasn't true to start with. So we've collapsed two morphisms down to one! This "collapse" is not a monomorphism in Cat.

(That's my summary of what you said, to help me remember this.)
