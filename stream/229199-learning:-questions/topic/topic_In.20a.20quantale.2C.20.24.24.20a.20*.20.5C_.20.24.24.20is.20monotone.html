Mike Shulman (Aug 19 2022 at 20:15):

Note that b≤cb\le cb≤c if and only if c=b∨cc = b\vee cc=b∨c. Thus, if b≤cb\le cb≤c then by the axiom given, a∗c=a∗(b∨c)=(a∗b)∨(a∗c)a \ast c = a\ast (b\vee c) = (a\ast b) \vee (a \ast c)a∗c=a∗(b∨c)=(a∗b)∨(a∗c), which is equivalent to a∗b≤a∗ca \ast b \le a \ast ca∗b≤a∗c.
