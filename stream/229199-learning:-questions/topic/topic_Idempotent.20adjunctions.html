Topic: Idempotent adjunctions

Leopold Schlicht (Jan 20 2022 at 21:10):

Let F⊣UF\dashv UF⊣U be an adjunction given by two functors F ⁣:C→DF\colon C\to DF:C→D and U ⁣:D→CU\colon D\to CU:D→C. Let EEE be the category of fixed points of this adjunction. Then the following conditions are equivalent, right?

  1. EEE is coreflective in DDD with coreflector F∘UF\circ UF∘U and EEE is reflective in CCC with reflector U∘FU\circ FU∘F.
  2. The fixed points on either side are exactly the images of FFF and GGG.

I think Remark 1.3 in [[idempotent adjunction]] says that (1) implies (2).

Which condition is easier to check in practice? I guess (2), because for proving (1) one probably needs to understand EEE concretely anyway.
