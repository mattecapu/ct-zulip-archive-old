Just to interface with this topic

This appears to me quite like adjunction, because both are "approximate inverse", but in fact it's not even a functor.

You conflated two things here. A map Γ:X→P(Y)\Gamma: X \to \mathcal{P}(Y)Γ:X→P(Y) is not inherently a functor. However, if we view powersets as partially ordered sets (which are a special case of categories), then the map Γ∗:P(X)→P(Y)\Gamma_*: \mathcal{P}(X) \to \mathcal{P}(Y)Γ∗​:P(X)→P(Y) sending A⊆XA \subseteq XA⊆X to ⋃x∈Af(x)\bigcup_{x \in A}f(x)⋃x∈A​f(x) is an order-preserving function (the corresponding special case of a functor). Observe that A⊆Γ+Γ∗(A)A \subseteq \Gamma^+ \Gamma_*(A)A⊆Γ+Γ∗​(A) and Γ∗Γ+(U)⊆U\Gamma_*\Gamma^+(U) \subseteq UΓ∗​Γ+(U)⊆U. Once you've looked into adjunctions some more, see if you can understand why this makes Γ+\Gamma^+Γ+ a right adjoint to Γ∗\Gamma_*Γ∗​. You can also work out what the relationship is between Γ∗\Gamma_*Γ∗​ and Γ−\Gamma^-Γ−.
