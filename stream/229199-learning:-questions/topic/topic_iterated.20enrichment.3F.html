Ben MacAdam (Mar 08 2021 at 18:58):

Mike Shulman said:

If VVV is closed symmetric monoidal, then to give a closed symmetric monoidal VVV-enriched category CCC with copowers is equivalent to giving a closed symmetric monoidal ordinary category CCC and a symmetric monoidal adjunction V⇄CV \rightleftarrows CV⇄C. The right adjoint can then be applied homwise to make any CCC-enriched category into a VVV-enriched category. And of course, symmetric monoidal adjunctions can be composed.

Mike Shulman said:

Unfortunately this is kind of folklore; I'm not sure of a reference.

I think this paper by Rory Lucyshyn-Wright is related: http://www.tac.mta.ca/tac/volumes/31/6/31-06.pdf, where he considers the case of a symmetric monoidal closed adjunction.

Bruno Gavranovic (Mar 10 2021 at 10:39):

Mike Shulman said:

The way to assign objects of WWW to objects of VVV is the right adjoint in the adjunction W⇄VW \rightleftarrows VW⇄V that's equivalent to making VVV a WWW-enriched closed monoidal category with copowers. In terms of the enrichment, that right adjoint sends x∈Vx\in Vx∈V to the WWW-enriched hom-object V(I,x)V(I,x)V(I,x), where III is the unit object for the monoidal structure of VVV. This is just like how any VVV-enriched category has an underlying Set\rm SetSet-enriched category.

Ah, I see - that makes a lot of sense!
