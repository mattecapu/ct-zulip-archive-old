Topic: Walking arrow in a 2-category

{% raw %}

Fawzi Hreiki (Feb 25 2021 at 22:16):

I don't know if this is a naive question, or if it even makes sense. In a 2-category, the comma objects (f/g)(f/g)(f/g) determine the 2-morphisms in the category (this is just their UMP). In Cat, the natural transformations are determined by the walking arrow 2=(0→1)2 = (0 \rightarrow 1)2=(0→1) which is a join (1⋆1)(1 \star 1)(1⋆1) and hence a particular kind of co-comma object f⋆gf\star gf⋆g. This is obviously reminiscent of the fact that 111 is strong generator in Set. Is there anyway to make sense of this? For example, what implications does it have on the internal logic of the 2-category?

Mike Shulman (Feb 26 2021 at 02:08):

It's true that 1 is a strong generator in Cat in a 2-categorical sense, i.e. if f:A→Bf:A\to Bf:A→B induces an equivalence of hom-categories K(1,A)→K(1,B)K(1,A) \to K(1,B)K(1,A)→K(1,B) then fff is an equivalence.
