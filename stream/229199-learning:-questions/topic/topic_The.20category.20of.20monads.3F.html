Topic: The category of monads?

{% raw %}

Anand Rao (May 12 2023 at 05:34):

For a given category C\mathcal{C}C, I was wondering if it is possible to construct a corresponding "category of monads" on C\mathcal{C}C. The objects of the category are monads on C\mathcal{C}C; given that a monad is a (endo-)functor with additional structure, it makes sense for the morphisms of the category to be natural transformations between the underlying functors with some additional properties. However, a naive approach along these lines presents some problems.

Let TTT and SSS be monads on C\mathcal{C}C, and let η\etaη be a natural transformation from TTT to SSS. Then the "unit" of the monad TTT, which is a natural transformation from 1C\mathbb{1}_{\mathcal{C}}1C​ to TTT, can be post-composed with η\etaη to give a natural transformation from 1C\mathbb{1}_{\mathcal{C}}1C​ to SSS; one can impose the condition that this natural transformation must be equal to the "unit" of the monad SSS. This kind of approach seems to fail for the "multiplication" of the monad. The difficulty lies in changing T2T^2T2 to S2S^2S2 using η\etaη -- one round of application of η\etaη transforms T2T^2T2 to STSTST, and it does not seem to be possible to simplify this further to S2S^2S2.

Is it possible to construct a sensible definition of the category of monads on C\mathcal{C}C along these lines?

Nathanael Arkor (May 12 2023 at 06:11):

The category of monads on a category C\mathcal CC is the category of monoids in the strict monoidal category [C,C][\mathcal C, \mathcal C][C,C]. If you expand the definitions, you should get the description you are looking for.

Dylan Braithwaite (May 12 2023 at 07:17):

Namely the extra step you need is that using the functoriality of SSS you get a natural transformation S(η):ST⇒SSS(\eta) : ST \Rightarrow SSS(η):ST⇒SS
