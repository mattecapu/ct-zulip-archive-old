John Baez (Feb 25 2022 at 00:04):

Physicists think a lot about T(T(M)). A point in M can represent the position qqq of a particle. A point (q,v)∈(q,v) \in(q,v)∈ T(M) then represents its position and velocity. In the Lagrangian approach to dynamics, the way position and velocity change over time is described by a vector field on T(T(M)). So, for any point (q,v)(q,v)(q,v) in T(M) we get a point in T(T(M)) that describes the rate at which position and velocity change over time. Usually a consistency condition holds, which says that the rate at which position changes is equal to the velocity!
