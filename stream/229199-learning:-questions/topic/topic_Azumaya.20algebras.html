So, there are two apparently equivalent definitions of an [[Azumaya algebra]] AAA over a commutative ring RRR:

  • an algebra AAA for which there's an algebra BBB such that B⊗AB \otimes AB⊗A is [[Morita equivalent]] to RRR.

  • an algebra AAA that's a [[separable algebra]] whose center is RRR.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 20:57):

The first definition is conceptually nice because there's a monoidal bicategory of

  • algebras over RRR
  • bimodules
  • bimodule homomorphisms

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 20:57):

where the monoidal structure comes from tensor product of algebras over RRR.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:00):

Then the first definition is saying the object AAA is Azumaya iff it's invertible: there's a BBB such that B⊗A≃IB \otimes A \simeq IB⊗A≃I in this monoidal bicategory.

(I believe this implies A⊗B≃IA \otimes B \simeq IA⊗B≃I, but I don't see why. I'd better check: if it doesn't, then there's a paper out there giving a bad definition of Azumaya algebra!)

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:02):

The second definition sounds less categorical, but actually a separable algebra is one where the multiplication A⊗RA→AA \otimes_R A \to AA⊗R​A→A has a section (that is, a right inverse) in the category of A,AA,AA,A-bimodules.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:04):

And this is very nice! First, it makes sense very generally: for any monoid object AAA in any monoidal category, we can say it's separable if its multiplication has right inverse in the category of A,AA,AA,A-biactions. Second, this right inverse makes AAA into a [[Frobenius monoid]].

John Baez (May 27 2023 at 00:36):

So, back to my original question. For any RRR-algebra AAA, the following are equivalent:

1) there's an algebra BBB such that B⊗RAB \otimes_R AB⊗R​A is Morita equivalent to RRR

2) AAA is separable and its center is RRR?

But what's the slickest proof? Can we find a proof that uses just string diagrams or other category-theoretic machinery?

To do this we should try to work in the symmetric monoidal bicategory Mod(R)\mathbf{Mod}(R)Mod(R) of

  • RRR-algebras,
  • bimodules between such algebras, and
  • bimodule homomorphisms

as much as possible, since that may let us generalize to other (sufficiently nice) symmetric monoidal bicategories.

Condition 1) is clearly phrased in terms of this monoidal bicategory Mod(R)\mathbf{Mod}(R)Mod(R): it's just saying that our object AAA has an object BBB that's a "tensor inverse" of AAA.

For condition 2), "separability" is nicely phrased in terms of this bicategory: it's saying that multiplication A⊗RA→AA \otimes_R A \to AA⊗R​A→A, viewed as homomorphism of A,AA,AA,A-bimodules, has a right inverse! But what about this business of the center of AAA being RRR? That sounds hard to express abstractly.

But then I remembered: any separable algebra is a Frobenius algebra! And for any Frobenius algebra AAA (over a field RRR, at least) there's a god-given way to project it down to its center! We comultiply A→A⊗RAA \to A \otimes_R AA→A⊗R​A, then use the symmetry to "switch" A⊗RA→A⊗RA A \otimes_R A \to A \otimes_R AA⊗R​A→A⊗R​A, and then multiply A⊗RA→AA \otimes_R A \to AA⊗R​A→A. This gives an idempotent p:A→Ap: A \to Ap:A→A that projects AAA down to its center. I showed why this works on page 84 here.

So, I think there's a nice way to say the center of AAA is just RRR when AAA is separable. We say this map p:A→Ap: A \to Ap:A→A equals the counit ϵ:A→R\epsilon: A \to Rϵ:A→R followed by the unit ι:R→A\iota : R \to Aι:R→A.

So in a symmetric monoidal category C\mathsf{C}C with equalizers preserved by taking tensor products with objects, we can talk about:

  • monoids in C\mathsf{C}C
  • left and right actions of a monoid A∈CA \in \mathsf{C}A∈C
  • the category of left actions of a monoid A∈CA \in \mathsf{C}A∈C, and similar for right actions
  • A,BA,BA,B-bimodules (or 'biactions') of a pair of monoids A,B∈CA,B \in \mathsf{C}A,B∈C
  • how to take an A,BA,BA,B-bimodule and a B,CB,CB,C-bimodule and tensor them over BBB to get an A,CA,CA,C-bimodule

and finally the monoidal bicategory of

  • monoids in C\mathsf{C}C
  • bimodules between such monoids
  • bimodule homomorphisms

This is a pretty general context for Azumaya--Brauer--Morita theory.
