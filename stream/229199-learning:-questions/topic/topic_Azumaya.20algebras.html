John Baez (May 26 2023 at 20:56):

So, there are two apparently equivalent definitions of an [[Azumaya algebra]] AAA over a commutative ring RRR:

  • an algebra AAA for which there's an algebra BBB such that B⊗AB \otimes AB⊗A is [[Morita equivalent]] to RRR.

  • an algebra AAA that's a [[separable algebra]] whose center is RRR.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 20:57):

The first definition is conceptually nice because there's a monoidal bicategory of

  • algebras over RRR
  • bimodules
  • bimodule homomorphisms

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 20:57):

where the monoidal structure comes from tensor product of algebras over RRR.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:00):

Then the first definition is saying the object AAA is Azumaya iff it's invertible: there's a BBB such that B⊗A≃IB \otimes A \simeq IB⊗A≃I in this monoidal bicategory.

(I believe this implies A⊗B≃IA \otimes B \simeq IA⊗B≃I, but I don't see why. I'd better check: if it doesn't, then there's a paper out there giving a bad definition of Azumaya algebra!)

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:02):

The second definition sounds less categorical, but actually a separable algebra is one where the multiplication A⊗RA→AA \otimes_R A \to AA⊗R​A→A has a section (that is, a right inverse) in the category of A,AA,AA,A-bimodules.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:04):

And this is very nice! First, it makes sense very generally: for any monoid object AAA in any monoidal category, we can say it's separable if its multiplication has right inverse in the category of A,AA,AA,A-biactions. Second, this right inverse makes AAA into a [[Frobenius monoid]].

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:05):

Proving this is easy and fun with string diagrams.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 21:06):

So, my question is whether there's an elegant proof that the two definitions of Azumaya algebra agree... a proof that might be generalized to other contexts.

Say AAA is the algebra of n×nn \times nn×n matrices with entries in RRR. Let eij∈Ae_{i j} \in Aeij​∈A be the elementary matrices. Then here's a map A↦A⊗RAA \mapsto A \otimes_R A A↦A⊗R​A that's a right inverse for multiplication:

eij↦1n∑k=1neik⊗ekj e_{ij} \mapsto \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^n e_{ik} \otimes e_{kj}eij​↦n1​∑k=1n​eik​⊗ekj​

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 22:35):

But notice, this only works if we can divide by nnn in RRR.

John Baez (May 26 2023 at 22:36):

So it turns out, for example, that the algebra of n×nn \times nn×n matrices with entries in a field of characteristic ppp is separable iff nnn is not divisible by ppp.
