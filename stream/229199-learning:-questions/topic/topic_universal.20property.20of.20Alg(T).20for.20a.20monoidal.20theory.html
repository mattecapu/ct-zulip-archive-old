Topic: universal property of Alg(T) for a monoidal theory

{% raw %}

Matt Earnshaw (Sep 20 2022 at 15:25):

The category of algebras Alg(T) \text{Alg}(T) Alg(T) for a Lawvere theory has a (2-) universal property: colimit preserving functors Alg(T)→B Alg(T) \to B Alg(T)→B correspond essentially uniquely to finite coproduct preserving functors Top→B T^{\text{op}} \to B Top→B. What is the universal property of the category of algebras for a monoidal theory (pro)?

Nathanael Arkor (Sep 20 2022 at 17:23):

If you're taking an algebra for a PRO V\mathcal VV to be a monoidal functor Vop→Set\mathcal V^{\mathrm{op}} \to \mathbf{Set}Vop→Set, then the category of algebras is the free monoidal cocompletion, and so a cocontinuous monoidal functor Alg(V)→V′\mathbf{Alg}(\mathcal V) \to \mathcal V'Alg(V)→V′ is equivalent to a monoidal functor V→V′\mathcal V \to \mathcal V'V→V′ (where V′\mathcal V'V′ is a cocomplete monoidal category).

Nathanael Arkor (Sep 20 2022 at 17:25):

(The universal property for algebraic theories is then the specialisation of the universal property for monoidal structure to cocartesian monoidal structure.)
