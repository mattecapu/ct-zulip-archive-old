Jules Hedges (Mar 29 2022 at 15:56):

My topology lecturer had a thing he called the "golden theorem", namely that a function between metric spaces is continuous in the ϵ−δ\epsilon-\deltaϵ−δ sense if and only if inverse images of open sets are open (where open = every point has an open ball in the set). That's the theorem that says that such a thing as topological spaces are even possible
