Nathaniel Virgo (Apr 04 2020 at 09:45):

Quick question: why are limits called limits? Is there some kind of connection or analogy to epsilon-delta type limits in analysis?

Matteo Capucci (Apr 04 2020 at 09:46):

There is, but I think historically the terminology comes from 'inductive limits' and 'projective limits' in algebra

Matteo Capucci (Apr 04 2020 at 09:48):

And since they are basically a limiting process, it's easy to see why they were called like this. I've no serious clue about the adjectives 'inductve' and 'projective' though.

Jules Hedges (Apr 04 2020 at 10:22):

Limits do capture some primal intuition about limiting processes on a sequence of objects. For example if XXX is a set then the "limit" of the sequence X,X2,X3,…X, X^2, X^3, \ldotsX,X2,X3,… should obviously be XωX^\omegaXω. I'd write that even if I'd never heard of category theory
