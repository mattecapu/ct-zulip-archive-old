Although some of the terminology and notation doesn't seem standard to me, I think the way it's written is correct. (I don't know what ⋄\diamond⋄ stands for either.)

The left cone undoubtedly means the category obtained by freely adjoining an initial object to the diagram category III.

Objects of the slice amount to tuples (c;{fi:c→X(i)}i∈I)(c; \{f_i: c \to X(i)\}_{i \in I})(c;{fi​:c→X(i)}i∈I​) where ccc is an object of CCC and fif_ifi​ is a morphism of CCC, one for each object iii of III, such that for any morphism g:i→jg: i \to jg:i→j in III, we have X(g)∘fi=fjX(g) \circ f_i = f_jX(g)∘fi​=fj​. A morphism of the slice, from (c;fi)(c; f_i)(c;fi​) to (d,gi)(d, g_i)(d,gi​), amount to morphisms h:c→dh: c \to dh:c→d such that fi=gi∘hf_i = g_i \circ hfi​=gi​∘h for all objects iii of III.
