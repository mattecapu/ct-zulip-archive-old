John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:35):

First, remember that there's a category Open(Petri)\mathsf{Open}(\mathsf{Petri})Open(Petri) of open Petri nets, a decorated cospan category where the objects are finite sets and a morphism is an open Petri net, meaning a cospan of finite sets

A→iS←oB A \xrightarrow{i} S \xleftarrow{o} BAi​So​B

together with a Petri net

s,t:T→N[S] s, t: T \to \mathbb{N}[S] s,t:T→N[S]

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:46):

(Actually this is a lie: a morphism is really an isomorphism class of open Petri nets; this is explained in various papers of mine, but I can also explain it here if asked.)

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:48):

Now, the original conjecture which some folks at the AMS MRC apparently actually proved was a complete classification of functors

F:Open(Petri)→BN F : \mathsf{Open}(\mathsf{Petri}) \to \mathsf{B} \mathbb{N} F:Open(Petri)→BN

where BN \mathsf{B} \mathbb{N} BN is the category with one object and natural numbers as morphisms, composition being addition of natural numbers.

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:49):

I'm too lazy to restate the classification until someone actually asks, but briefly it says every such functor FFF is a finite sum of functors of a very specific sort, called

Fm,n:Open(Petri)→BN F_{m,n} : \mathsf{Open}(\mathsf{Petri}) \to \mathsf{B} \mathbb{N} Fm,n​:Open(Petri)→BN

which count the transitions of various kinds (depending on two natural numbers m,nm, nm,n).

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:54):

Moreover every such finite sum gives a functor

F:Open(Petri)→BN F : \mathsf{Open}(\mathsf{Petri}) \to \mathsf{B} \mathbb{N} F:Open(Petri)→BN

So, these functors are completely understood. And they're important because, intuitively speaking, they are the "additive invariants of open Petri nets".

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:55):

(Invariants taking natural numbers as values, I mean.)

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:56):

Now for the next question! There's a subcategory of Open(Petri)\mathsf{Open}(\mathsf{Petri})Open(Petri) consisting of (isomorphism classes of) open Petri nets

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:57):

A→iS←oB, A \xrightarrow{i} S \xleftarrow{o} B, Ai​So​B,

s,t:T→N[S] s, t: T \to \mathbb{N}[S] s,t:T→N[S]

such that the functions s,ts, ts,t are monic.

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 04:58):

Let's call this subcategory Mon(Petri)\mathsf{Mon}(\mathsf{Petri})Mon(Petri), at least until we come up with a better name.

The question is, what are all the functors

F:Mon(Petri)→BN F : \mathsf{Mon}(\mathsf{Petri}) \to \mathsf{B} \mathbb{N} F:Mon(Petri)→BN ?

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 05:04):

All the finite sums functors Fm,nF_{m,n}Fm,n​ restrict from Open(Petri)\mathsf{Open}(\mathsf{Petri})Open(Petri) to Mon(Petri)\mathsf{Mon}(\mathsf{Petri})Mon(Petri), of course, and this restriction process is one-to-one (I claim), so the classification we had before is part of the classification we want.

John Baez (Jun 06 2022 at 05:05):
