6th Annual International Conference on Applied Category Theory (ACT2023)

July 31 - August 4, 2023

https://act2023.github.io/

The Sixth International Conference on Applied Category Theory will take place at the University of Maryland from 31 July to 4 August 2023, preceded by the Adjoint School 2023 from 24 to 28 July. This conference follows previous events at Strathclyde (UK), Cambridge (UK), Cambridge (MA), Oxford (UK) and Leiden (NL).

John Baez (Feb 05 2023 at 00:23):

Applied category theory is important to a growing community of researchers who study computer science, logic, engineering, physics, biology, chemistry, social science, systems, linguistics and other subjects using category-theoretic tools. The background and experience of our members is as varied as the systems being studied. The goal of the Applied Category Theory conference series is to bring researchers together, strengthen the applied category theory community, disseminate the latest results, and facilitate further development of the field.

John Baez (Apr 04 2023 at 20:54):

Some news about submitting your talks to ACT2023:

Submission formats:

  1. Conference Papers should present original, high-quality work in the style of a computer science conference paper (up to 12 pages, not counting the bibliography; more detailed parts of proofs may be included in an appendix for the convenience of the reviewers). Such submissions should not be an abridged version of an existing journal article although pre-submission arXiv preprints are permitted. These submissions will be adjudicated for both a talk and publication in the conference proceedings.

  2. Talk proposals not to be published in the proceedings, e.g. about work accepted/submitted/published elsewhere, should be submitted as abstracts, one or two pages long. Authors are encouraged to include links to any full versions of their papers, preprints or manuscripts. The purpose of the abstract is to provide a basis for determining the topics and quality of the anticipated presentation.

  3. Software demonstration proposals should also be submitted as abstracts, one or two pages. The purpose of the abstract is to provide the program committee with enough information to assess the content of the demonstration.

The original conference papers will ultimately be published with EPTCS, and authors are advised to use the style files available at style.eptcs.org.

DATES AND DEADLINES

Please submit your papers and talk proposals at: OpenReview
<https://openreview.net/group?id=AppliedCategoryTheory.org/ACT/2023/Conference>.

You will need to create an account or log in. Please tell OpenReview as
much as you can about yourself, and your past papers, because it uses this to automatically calculate conflict of interest. (Reviewing is
single-blind, and we are not making public the reviews, reviewer names, the discussions nor the list of under-review submissions. This is the same as previous instances of ACT.) The exact deadline time on these dates is given by anywhere on earth <http://www.ieee802.org/16/aoe.html> (AoE).

Papers and Abstracts Due 3 May 2023
Author Notification 7 June 2023
Conference 31 July – 4 August 2023

Limited financial support will be available for travel. Priority will be
given to people with financial need and those giving presentations. We are also aware that not everyone is able to travel to the US, e.g. for visa
reasons, and we will accommodate that in the programme via remote
participation etc. Please contact the organisers for more information.

Cole Comfort (May 06 2023 at 14:16):

My concern is more that if there are no clear rules about how the program committee is decided, then a professor can have disproportionate influence on the community by appointing their own students to the program committee: elevating themselves, their students and their research disproportionately.

This is especially bad for students whose supervisors are not involved in the conference, because then, not only will they be less likely to subreview papers, but their influence will be made even lower in proportion to groups where multiple members are on the committee.

I find it kind of strange, that to my knowledge, last year in my research group no one subreviewed any papers for the conference, but my fellow PhD students at other research groups where most likely sending papers to their colleagues to subreview for them! Admittedly, this may be a problem with the structure of our group, but I think it would be good if this sort of thing could be mediated by the senior members of the comittee.

This seems kind of exclusionary. Given that the conference is supposed to broadly represent applied category theory as a discipline, I think it would be better if the criteria were made clear. And moreover, I think it is important that people from all groups who are working on applied category theory are equally represented in deciding which papers are accepted, and thus, to shape the future of the community.

Of course, these things are usually decided by professors behind closed doors, which I am not privy to as a PhD student, but the fact that there are PhD students on the program comittee is very unusual.

This is just my opinion as a PhD student, and I have no right to have any influence over the direction of the conference, of course. I am not trying to be critical or mean or anything and I hope that this conference goes really well and is great fun as usual! :)
