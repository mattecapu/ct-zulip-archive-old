Ralph Sarkis (Dec 14 2022 at 18:00):

Not an answer (but maybe a helpful discussion). Just to make sure I understand, we have A≤BA \leq BA≤B implies the language recognized by AAA (denoted by L(A)L(A)L(A)) is included in L(B)L(B)L(B), but not necessarily the converse. That is, we might have L(A)⊆L(B)L(A) \subseteq L(B)L(A)⊆L(B) but not A≤BA\leq BA≤B.

So we know the language recognized by the meet of A1A_1A1​ and A2A_2A2​ (if it exists) is included in the intersection of the languages. This suggests your construction, but the failure of the converse above does not help confirming your construction is the right one.
