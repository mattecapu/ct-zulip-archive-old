John Baez (Jun 10 2023 at 23:27):

By the way, one reason I gave up on this thread is that I realized that this version of Wedderburn's theorem is a bit limited:

Wedderburn for the symmetric monoidal closed category A\mathsf{A}A: every simple monoid R∈AR \in \mathsf{A}R∈A is isomorphic to one of the form D⊗(I⊕n⊸I⊕n)D \otimes (I^{\oplus n} \multimap I^{\oplus n})D⊗(I⊕n⊸I⊕n) where DDD is some division monoid.

For example, if A\mathsf{A}A is the category of complex vector bundles over a topological space, a monoid in A\mathsf{A}A is an algebra bundle, and I believe there are simple monoids not of this form, coming from algebra bundles that are locally isomorphic to trivial bundles of matrix algebras, but not globally.

So, I think we should expect "Wedderburn for A\mathsf{A}A" to hold in the above form only under some conditions, e.g. when all projective objects are free. It would still be interesting (to me) to find a bunch of symmetric monoidal closed categories for which this limited form of Wedderburn holds.... or to state Wedderburn in a more general way. But I decided to go in another direction, which took me to separable algebras and Azumaya algebras.

Mike Shulman (Jun 12 2023 at 20:23):

But conjectures A and B should be pretty straightforrward enriched category theory, even with Ab replaced by some other Benabou cosmos. E.g. for conjecture B, you can think of RModR\mathsf{Mod}RMod as an A\mathsf{A}A-enriched presheaf category on RRR regarded as a one-object A\mathsf{A}A-enriched category. Then to get the Ab-enrichment, you can use the fact that a monoidal Ab-enriched category is the same as an ordinary monoidal category A\mathcal{A}A with a monoidal adjunction Ab⇄A\mathrm{Ab} \rightleftarrows \mathcal{A}Ab⇄A, and apply change-of-enrichment along the right adjoint. That right adjoint sends an object X∈AX\in \mathsf{A}X∈A to the Ab-enriched hom hom⁡A(I,X)\hom_{\mathsf{A}}(I,X)homA​(I,X), where III is the monoidal unit of A\mathsf{A}A.
