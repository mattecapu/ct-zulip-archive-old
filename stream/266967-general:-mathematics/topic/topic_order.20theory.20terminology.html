Max New (Apr 12 2023 at 18:01):

Does anyone know a word for this concept in order theory? I have a monotone function f:P→Qf : P \to Qf:P→Q and it is not just surjective but for any q≤q′q \leq q'q≤q′ in QQQ there exists p≤p′p \leq p'p≤p′ in PPP such that f(p)=qf(p) = qf(p)=q and f(p′)=q′f(p') = q'f(p′)=q′. It's not the same as being a full functor because the ppp depends on both qqq and q′q'q′.

Max New (Apr 12 2023 at 18:04):

In fact my example is not full: there are cases where f(p)≤f(p′)f(p) \leq f(p')f(p)≤f(p′) but not p≤p′p \leq p'p≤p′

Amar Hadzihasanovic (Apr 12 2023 at 19:28):

This is equivalent to saying that

  • fff is surjective, and
  • fff maps lower sets to lower sets.

Indeed, take q≤q′q \leq q'q≤q′, by surjectiveness there exists p′p'p′ with q′=f(p′)q' = f(p')q′=f(p′), and since the image of the lower set of p′p'p′ is the lower set of f(p′)f(p')f(p′), there must exist p≤p′p \leq p'p≤p′ such that f(p)=qf(p) = qf(p)=q.

I would simply call the second condition being a closed map, since lower sets are the closed sets for the Alexandrov topology on the poset.
