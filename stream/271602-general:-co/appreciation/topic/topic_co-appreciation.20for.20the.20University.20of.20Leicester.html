Jules Hedges (Jun 13 2021 at 11:32):

In theory, the difference is that "fired" means you're kicked out of your job for doing certain extremely bad things and then somebody else is hired into your job, whereas "made redundant" means that your job itself no longer exists, usually for restructuring/financial reasons. In practice, targeted redunancies are common, for example UCU representatives are being made redundant much more frequently than everyone else. (Which as far as I know is illegal but employers generally get away with it)

Joe Moeller (Jun 14 2021 at 13:17):

Georgios Bakirtzis (Jun 17 2021 at 02:32):

@Joe Moeller FWIW that is not what furloughed means. Furloughed usually means the company is forcing you to have something like forced leave without pay but it's never "if they could bring you back", you don't lose or often it's not the case that you are losing your job when you are furloughed. If the company can't recover after a furloughed period THEN it might move to letting people go.
