The whole setup of GGG makes me think of the nerve construction... could this be its version for graphs? In the nerve construction, you associate a simplicial set (= a presheaf on the simplex category) to a small category (= a graph + composition). You do so by considering paths of composable edges of length 0, 1, 2, and so on. Since a simplicial set also has face (they go n→n+1n \to n+1n→n+1)/degeneracies (they go n+1→nn+1 \to nn+1→n) maps, one has also to specify those. It's combinatorially fiddly, but basically they amount to either add identities somewhere in the path (these are face maps, since they lengthen the path), forget the first/last morphism (these are degeneracies d0d_0d0​ and dnd_ndn​) or compose the two consecutive middle maps (these are degeneracies between d1d_1d1​ and dn−1d_{n-1}dn−1​).
Don't take my word for it, check the nlab where everything is worked out in more detail (so I won't repeat everything here).
The moral is, to each category one can associate a simplicial set which track the way you can move around it.
The cool thing is, there's a canonical way to turn simplicial things into complexes, the Dold-Kan correspondence. It's easiest when you have simplicial abelian groups (or any good abelian category, I guess KKK-algebras are good too) instead of simplicial sets, i.e. if the presheaf on the simplex category actually lands in AbAbAb (or, again, your preferred abelian cat). Then by waving a magic wand you automagically get a complex out of this.
I think it's not unreasonable to think that your construction amounts to take a graph GGG, build its free category F(G)F(G)F(G), build its nerve N(F(G))N(F(G))N(F(G)), then take free KKK-algebras to get a simplicial KKK-algebra KN(F(G))K^{N(F(G))}KN(F(G)) and run the Dold-Kan construction.
