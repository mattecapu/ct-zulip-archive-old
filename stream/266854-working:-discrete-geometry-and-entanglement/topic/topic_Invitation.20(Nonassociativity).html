The associative product of simplicial cochains
{}
α β=α(1,2) β(2,3) e1,2,3\alpha\,\beta = \alpha(1,2)\,\beta(2,3)\,e^{1,2,3}αβ=α(1,2)β(2,3)e1,2,3

Eric Forgy (Jan 11 2021 at 07:37):

However, if you put two simplicial 2-cochains together (forming a 2-diamond), you get
{}
α β=[α(1,2) β(2,3)−α(1,2′) β(2′,3)](e1,2,3−e1,2′,3).\begin{aligned}\alpha\,\beta = \left[\alpha(1,2)\,\beta(2,3)-\alpha(1,2')\,\beta(2',3)\right] (e^{1,2,3} - e^{1,2',3}).\end{aligned}αβ=[α(1,2)β(2,3)−α(1,2′)β(2′,3)](e1,2,3−e1,2′,3).​
{}
This is associative and approximates the continuum wedge product.
