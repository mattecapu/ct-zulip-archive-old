Topic: Formal properties of the bar construction

{% raw %}

Patrick Nicodemus (Dec 24 2021 at 11:57):

There's an interesting discussion here with a few unresolved questions. https://nforum.ncatlab.org/discussion/6644/bar-construction-as-a-cofibrant-replacement/
I would like to resurface this topic and ask if anyone has good references to these questions.
The bar construction is omnipresent, I am looking for concrete references on its properties in modern language. Here is a more specific question. Let C\mathcal{C}C be an arbitrary category equipped with a comonad GGG.
The bar construction associates to each object in C\mathcal{C}C a simplicial GGG-coalgebra.

I would like to know if there is any model category structure on the category of simplicial objects in C\mathcal{C}C which is somehow derived from GGG, or in which certain simplicial objects defined in terms of GGG turn out to have good properties (for example the simplicial GGG-coalgebras are cofibrant.) In what sense can we say the bar construction is a cofibrant replacement?

As an example take C\mathcal{C}C to be groups or abelian groups and GGG the forgetful-free functor of the adjunction, in this case one replaces a given Abelian group by a free resolution; do the free simplicial Abelian groups play an important role with respect to the usual model structure on SAB? I think they are cofibrant but I don't know a source for this fact.

Patrick Nicodemus (Dec 24 2021 at 12:00):

Opening things up more generally, what are your favorite theoretical characterizations of the bar construction and its properties? In whatever formalism. What context do you think it's best to view it in?

Zhen Lin Low (Dec 24 2021 at 12:16):

I don't think it works in such great generality. Actually, I think it doesn't even always work for the comonad associated with the free–forgetful adjunction of a category of algebras for a finitary algebraic theory – there is a technical condition on the (morphisms that would be) trivial cofibrations, which is implied by the existence of fibrant replacements and fibrant path objects, but is not automatic.
