Hello,

I'd like to advertise a funded PhD opportunity in the CS theory group at Swansea University, to be supervised by myself. The provisional topic would be exploring connections between (linear) λ-calculi and automata theory, hopefully using semantic methods. The official adverts detailing some possible research directions is there https://www.swansea.ac.uk/postgraduate/scholarships/research/computer-science-epsrc-su-phd-comparing-2022-rs125.php (the deadline for application is July 29th, which is relatively soon)

(The connections to CT here would be mainly via the categorical semantics of linear logic. With @Nguyễn Lê Thành Dũng we had some successes proving some results in this area by freely building up some simple categories via a bunch of free constructions and noticing that we could equip those with a smcc structure and relate this to known transducers models; see in particular https://nguyentito.eu/thesis.pdf chapters 4&6. Among other things to be done that are somewhat related to CT, we think there are similar opportunities to use semantic evaluation arguments based on the non-commutative version of the Int\mathsf{Int}Int construction)

(Also I feel I should plug Eike Neumann who has also a call open with the same funding scheme about reachability problems and exact real number computability with the same sort of deadline/arrangement that sounds very interesting :)
https://www.swansea.ac.uk/postgraduate/scholarships/research/computer-science-epsrc-su-phd-robust-2022-rs122.php)

If that sounds enticing, informal enquiries are very welcome! I can be reached via email (p.r.a.pradic@swansea.ac.uk), or one can try PMing me here if that is easier (I should also be around at ACT in Glasgow this year).
Also happy to discuss other project ideas I would be suited to supervise (I suppose one can get some idea of what other areas I work in by browsing through my personal webpage https://cs-web.swan.ac.uk/~pierrepradic/ but I mostly do logic, automata and type theory I guess), and feel free to forward to anyone who might be interested.
