Fabrizio Genovese (Jun 30 2021 at 15:06):

In any case, the very same fact that ML is reaching such important results by essentially heuristic means is philosophically interesting. Indeed, the idea of science as a completely formal thing is quite recent: We used to build entire cathedrals, pyramids and tons of other amazing stuff purely heuristically. Architects back then had no clue about what a tensor of inertia was, and still... This is to say that I am not necessarily a fan of the motto "ML people have no clue about why their stuff works." I think they have, it's just that their intuition does not respect what we people in hard sciences usually mean when we say that we know something.
