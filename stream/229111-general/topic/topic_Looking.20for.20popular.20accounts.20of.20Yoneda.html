From one side, the sculpture looks like an elephant; from another, it looks like two giraffes. But neither angle gives a full description. To really understand the sculpture, we should view it from all possible vantage points. The same idea holds in category theory: more vantage points give more information. all vantage points give all information.

This is the essence of the Yoneda perspective mentioned above, and is one reason why categorically-minded mathematicians place so much emphasis on morphisms, commuting diagrams, universal properties, and the like. (Have you noticed?) It's all about relationships!
